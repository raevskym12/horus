Horus

A rainforest drone that identifies high value medicinal plants.
We facilitate sustainable harvesting by identifying areas yielding the highest profits.


Solution


The average acre of rainforest has an expected lifetime yield of $460 if used for unsustainable timbering and agriculture, or $2,400 if used for sustainable harvesting of edible and medicinal plants.

We use machine learning methods to identify which land areas yield the highest payoffs, and facilitate investment by selling this information to pharmaceuticals and grocers, thereby preventing deforestation.

Impact

25% of all prescription drugs and over 70% of all cancer treatment medications originate from the rainforest. However, less than 1% of all rainforest plant species have ever been studied.

As we lose 34 million acres of rainforest per year, we also loose an infinite gold mine of life-saving medicine, a trend that Horus is fighting to curb.
